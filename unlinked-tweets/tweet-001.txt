yes, this post is literally a custom copy of Minecraft Classic that I call Minicraft

it works by abusing X's compose-link trick (shoutout @rebane2001): tweet 2's link opens a separate, easier-to-interact-with viewer (the decoder). the game itself rides in the rest of the thread as raw compressed text. this is probably the most compressed copy of Minecraft Classic but.. even so, it takes 20 tweets to hold the whole game.

how this thread is laid out:
tweet 2 is the decoder, a tiny self-contained page hidden in a link.
tweets 3 to 20 are the game itself, posted directly as raw compressed base64 chunks.

to play:
1) open tweet 2's link, copy the whole  data:text/html;base64,...  line it shows, and paste it into your browser's address bar. that opens the decoder (or save that line as decoder.html and open it).
2) in the decoder, hit 'auto-fill from github' (it grabs every part for you), or paste tweets 3-on in yourself, then hit Play. Minecraft runs right in your browser, nothing leaves your machine.
